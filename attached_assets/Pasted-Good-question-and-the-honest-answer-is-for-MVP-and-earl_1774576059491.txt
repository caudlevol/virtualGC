Good question — and the honest answer is: for MVP and early testing, no. For production with real agents and clients, yes, it will become a problem eventually — but not immediately.
Here is how to think about it clearly:
Breaking Down What "5 Per Minute" Actually Means
In your use case, one "token" = one visualization render. So 5 per minute means a maximum of 5 users can request a visualization image within the same 60-second window across your entire account. At $0.12 per token, that is $0.12 per render.
For your current stage: You are building and testing. You are not going to have 5 simultaneous users hammering the visualization button in the same minute. This rate limit is completely fine for development, demos, and your first 20–50 beta users.
Where it becomes a real problem: Once you have agents actively using the tool during showings — which typically cluster between 10am–1pm and 4pm–7pm on weekends — you could realistically hit the cap. Imagine 10 agents each showing a house at 11am on a Saturday and each client hitting "Visualize." That is a 429 rate limit error at the worst possible moment — mid-demo in front of a buyer.
What To Do About It
Right now — build in a graceful queue. In your Replit code, when a 429 rate limit error comes back from ReImage AI, do not show an error. Instead show: "Your visualization is being generated — it will be ready in about 60 seconds." Then retry automatically after 60 seconds. This turns a hard failure into a minor delay and no user ever knows the limit was hit.
Before you go live — contact ReImage AI directly. You are a B2B customer building on their API. Email them at contact@reimage.ai and ask two things: (1) what does a higher rate limit tier cost, and (2) is there a monthly plan vs. pay-per-token option. Early-stage API companies almost always have a negotiable commercial tier that is not published on the dashboard. You have leverage here — you are building a product that will drive recurring volume for them.
Pricing math for your model: At $0.12 per render and your planned pricing of ~$39–$79/month per agent, you need to make sure visualization does not become a margin killer. If an agent on your Pro plan generates 50 visualizations per month, that is $6 in ReImage AI costs against $39 in revenue — totally fine at 15% COGS. If a heavy user generates 200/month, that is $24 against $39 — that is a problem. Cap visualizations per tier (e.g., 20/month on Basic, 100/month on Pro, unlimited on Brokerage) to protect your margins before you launch.
Bottom Line
The 5/minute rate limit is a free tier constraint, not a hard ceiling on the platform. It is not a blocker for where you are today. Build the graceful retry, cap renders per subscription tier, and negotiate a commercial rate before you onboard your first paying cohort. That sequence protects you without slowing down the build.
